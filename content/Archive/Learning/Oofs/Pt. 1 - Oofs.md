I realize that a lot of my learning for Start-Ups happens in a specific process:<br><br>

1. I start by putting information into ChatGPT and asking it to summarize it in bullet points.<br>
2. Then, I go over those bullet points.<br>
3. After that, I re-input the information into my head, going over it from A to Z.<br>
4. Finally, I try things on my own.<br><br>

If this is the case, I want to show the entire process, not just the fourth step where I try things independently. From now on, I plan to post my ChatGPT notes alongside the work I create with someone else (step 3) and the projects I complete on my own (step 4).<br><br>

I've also realized something important about learning: it’s better to tackle a lot of things simultaneously over a long period, rather than focusing on one thing at a time for a short period. For example, I used to follow this learning format:<br><br>

Day 1: Finish all of Math<br>
Day 2: Finish all of Social Studies<br>
Day 3: Finish all of Science<br><br>

However, I’ve found that it works better—especially with coding and business—if I spread out the learning:<br><br>

Day 1: Pt. 1/3 Math, Pt. 1/3 Social Studies, Pt. 1/3 Science<br>
Day 2: Pt. 2/3 Math, Pt. 2/3 Social Studies, Pt. 2/3 Science<br>
Day 3: Pt. 3/3 Math, Pt. 3/3 Social Studies, Pt. 3/3 Science<br><br>

After completing some general courses for Marketing Start Ups, I’m thinking of holding off on further courses unless they’re project-based. I’m most likely going to stick to making projects instead.
